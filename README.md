# WaterProject
The project started by researching SPH fluid simulation and Marching Cubes surface generation. After understanding how particles generate the water mesh, I focused on optimizing the simulation and adding gameplay interactions.

The first additions were color support and water mixing, allowing multiple water bodies to blend together when they interact. After that I implemented swimming mechanics for a specific character, including buoyancy, movement in water and underwater camera transitions.

The final stage focused on interaction systems such as floating objects, collision filtering and camera behaviour. This allowed some objects and characters to interact differently with the water depending on their gameplay role.

## Script Description

## FluidSim
FluidSim is the core simulation script. It manages particle positions, velocities, densities and pressure calculations using SPH fluid simulation. It also handles particle collisions, simulation updates and density map generation for rendering.

## Spawner3D
Spawner3D creates the initial particle setup for the simulation. It generates particles inside configurable spawn regions and assigns starting positions, velocities and colors.

## MarchingCubes
MarchingCubes converts the particle density field into a renderable mesh. It uses a compute shader to generate triangles based on density values stored in a 3D texture.

## MarchingCubesFluidRenderer
MarchingCubesFluidRenderer is responsible for drawing the generated water mesh. It receives the triangle data from the Marching Cubes system and renders the final water surface in the scene.

## WaterMixingTrigger
WaterMixingTrigger detects when two water bodies interact and starts the color mixing process. It allows separate water simulations to gradually blend into a shared color.

## FloatingObject
FloatingObject allows objects to float on the water surface. It applies upward forces and drag when an object enters the water, creating a simple buoyancy effect.

## Water Shaders
The water shaders handle the visual appearance of the fluid. They render particle colors, mixed water colors and the final surface appearance generated by the Marching Cubes mesh.
